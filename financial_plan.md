## Earnings — The Honest Truth

### YouTube Shorts RPM (Revenue per 1,000 views)
- Kids content Shorts: **$0.01–$0.05 per 1,000 views**
- This is extremely low — COPPA limits what ads can run on kids content
- Long-form videos (10+ min) earn 10–20x more per view

### What you need to even start earning:
- **500 subscribers** + **3 million Shorts views in 90 days** → YouTube Partner Program

### Realistic view projections (new channel, 2 videos/day):

| Month | Videos Live | Est. Total Views | Monthly Revenue |
|-------|------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 1 | 60 | 5,000–20,000 | $0 |
| 2 | 120 | 20,000–80,000 | $0 |
| 3 | 180 | 50,000–200,000 | $0 (not monetized) |
| 6 | 360 | 200,000–800,000 | $0–$20 |
| 9 | 540 | 500,000–2,000,000 | $10–$80 |
| 12 | 720 | 1M–5M | $30–$200 |

### Break-even point
- Monthly costs: **$57**
- To break even on Shorts ads alone: **1–5 million views/month**
- Realistic timeline to break even: **12–18 months** if the channel grows well

## The Real Money (not Shorts ads)

| Revenue stream | When | Realistic monthly |

| Long-form compilations (10–20 min) | Month 6+ | $50–$500 |

> Long-form is the key unlock — take 5 Shorts, stitch into a 10-min compilation, upload as regular video. Same content, 10x the ad revenue.

## Year 1 Honest Scenario

### Best case (channel hits a viral video early)
- Break even by month 8
- $500–$1,000/month by end of year 1

### Realistic case (steady growth, no viral hits)
- Break even by month 14–16
- $100–$300/month by end of year 1

### Worst case (no traction)
- Never breaks even on ads alone
- Total year 1 loss: ~$684 ($57 × 12)

---

## Year 1 Total Cost
- $57/month × 12 months = **$684**
- This is your maximum downside — you own 700+ videos at the end regardless

---

## What actually determines success

1. **One viral video changes everything** — kids content goes viral randomly
2. **Consistency beats quality** — 700 videos > 70 perfect videos
3. **Long-form compilations** — the real AdSense money is here, not Shorts
4. **Merch early** — even $100/month from Gumroad printables covers half your costs

## Verdict

| Question | Answer |

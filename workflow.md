# YouTube Kids Shorts — Exact Workflow (from Instagram Reel)

## The Creator's Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1 — Find Inspiration
- Go to **Nunu TV** on YouTube (top kids nursery rhyme channel)
- Study their format: visuals, pacing, rhyme style, length

### Step 2 — Generate Script with ChatGPT
- Open ChatGPT
- Ask for a **1-minute breakdown of a kids short nursery rhyme video**
- Copy the full output

**Example prompt to use:**
> "Give me a 1-minute breakdown of a kids YouTube Short nursery rhyme video. Include scene-by-scene description, voiceover script, and timing for each scene. Style should be similar to Nunu TV — bright, simple, fun for toddlers aged 2–6."

### Step 3 — Paste into Viewmax Studio
- Open **Viewmax Studio** (AI video generator)
- Paste the full ChatGPT output into the tool
- Let it generate the video automatically

### Step 4 — Set English Audio + Captions
- Before exporting, make sure:
  - Audio language is set to **English**
  - **Captions/subtitles** are enabled
- Export the final Short

### Step 5 — Upload to YouTube
- Upload as a YouTube Short (vertical, under 60 sec)
- Set **Made for Kids** flag in YouTube Studio
- Add simple searchable title, tags, description

## Key Notes
- The entire video is AI-generated — no camera, no face, no manual editing
- English audio + captions are essential (accessibility + YouTube SEO)
- Nunu TV is the benchmark to model content after
- One workflow cycle can produce a finished Short in under 1 hour
